What Is Geoguessr? #

Geoguessr is an online video game. Geoguessr takes you around the world, as a challenge to find the location of an interactive Panorama.

Geoguessr is a freemium. Let’s define what a freemium is:

A combination of the words “free” and “premium,” freemium […] offers basic features of a product or service to users at no cost and charges a premium for supplemental […] features. Investopedia

In its paid version, the game uses imagery from Google Street View. Google Street View provides interactive Panoramas from locations all around the world.

Google takes most Panoramas from cars. On rarer occasions, they also take Panoramas from bicycles or on foot. Individuals can also submit their Photo Spheres.

As a freemium, Geoguessr offers unlimited features to subscribers.

Classic Modes #

Classic Modes are the original Geoguessr modes.

Explorer #

In Explorer, you can play a Map for each of the countries available in Geoguessr.

The maximum score in a game of Geoguessr is 25,000, with 5,000 points per round, depending on the distance to the location.

You can get a medal for each country played, depending on your score.

MedalMinimum score
Gold medal22,500
Silver medal15,000
Bronze medal5,000
Streaks #

In Streaks, you can play two different modes: Country Streaks and US State Streaks.

In each round, you must identify the right country or US State. The higher the number of countries or US States identified without an error, the better. One wrong answer ends the streak.

The Daily Challenge #

In The Daily Challenge, you are offered a new game everyday. The game is the same for all Geoguessr players.

All Geoguessr players compete for the highest score in the shortest time.

👉
In The Daily Challenge, you play for 3 minutes per round. Moving, panning and zooming are allowed. You cannot change these settings.
Pro Leagues #

In Pro Leagues, you can create or join leagues to compete with friends.

Map Maker #

Map Maker allows you to create your own Geoguessr Map. Let’s discover how to use this mode.

  1. Open Geoguessr.

  2. Click Classic.

  3. Click Map Maker.

  4. Select your locations using the method of your choice.

    • You can use Handpicked Locations .
    👉
    You must choose at least 5 locations.
    • You can use Polygonal Regions .

  5. Enter your Map name.

  6. Enter your Map description.

  7. Select your Map avatar.

    By default, the avatar is the following:

    1. Select a background.
    2. Select a ground.
    3. Select a landscape.
    4. Select decorations.

  8. Select Visible to others.

  9. Select Highlight on profile page.

  10. Click Create map.

You have created your very own Geoguessr Map! Congratulations. You can now share it with friends.

Career Modes #

Career Modes mode allows you to compete against other unknown players.

According to your score each game, your Career Modes ranking evolves and you can win trophies.

Battle Royale Countries #

In Battle Royale Countries, you compete to guess the country, not a specific location.

You can click anywhere within the borders of a country. Each round lasts a minute and a half. There are as many rounds as there are players, up to 10.

In each round, you have 3 guesses. All players can see the countries wrongly guessed by other players. The correct country reveals itself after you guess right or fail.

Meaning: Matthewcdr09 has been eliminated due to 3 wrong guesses. OngoGablogian has made one wrong guess with Tunisia. Vorobey and Vargaton2 have made no guess. Tunisia, Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Morocco and Egypt have already been guessed and are wrong. These countries may have been guessed by already qualified players.

Battle Royale Distance #

In Battle Royale Distance, you compete to guess your location, and the player who guesses the farthest is eliminated.

Each round lasts a minute. There are as many rounds as there are players, up to 10.

Every time you guess, you are shown the distance between your guess and your two closest competitors. You are only shown the distance between your guess and the next best guess if you are the closest to the location. You can try to get closer to the location by making more guesses. However, you will have to wait 5 seconds between each guess.

Meaning: the best guess by Adz is 58 km away from the second best guess by lourxrdes. Jean-Erik will be eliminated if they cannot guess closer than at least Vlad Dr before the time is up.

Meaning: the second best guess by Adz is 62 km away from the best guess by lourxrdes. It is 102 km from the third best guess by Jean-Erik. Jari P Niemela will be eliminated if they cannot guess closer than at least Kanush Karan before the time is up.

City Streaks #

In City Streaks, you compete to guess your location among a number of proposed cities.

A game lasts 3 minutes. There are as many rounds as there are players, up to 10.

You must guess the maximum number of cities in a row in the allotted time.

Meaning: Adz has guessed right one city and has 3 remaining guesses. tintinho has guessed 4 cities and has one remaining guess before losing.

Duels #

In Duels, you compete against another unknown player to guess your location.

You begin the game with 6,000 points. In each round, you and another player must guess the location of a Panorama. The score of the losing guess is subtracted from the score of the winning guess. The result is subtracted from the points total of the loser of the round.

Play with Friends Mode #

Play with Friends Mode allows you to create parties to compete against your friends.

In a Geoguessr party, you can play with one to 9 other players in a selected game. The other players access the party through a shareable link.

The games are the same as in Career Modes mode, but you can play them with players you choose. The games are:

  • Battle Royale Countries

  • Battle Royale Distance

  • City Streaks

  • Duels

  • Team Duels

    You can play Team Duels the same way as Duels. However, you and your friends have to be part of either a Team Red or a Team Blue. The closest guess per team wins.

  • Bulls Eye

    You can play Bulls Eye collaboratively with two to 5 other players. You have to guess your location by drawing a circle on the Map. If the location is inside the perimeter of the circle, the team wins points.

    When one player plays, the other players know of their score. You are not timed and play continues until all players submit a guess.

Where the heck am I?! #

Now that you are more familiar with Geoguessr, let’s see how to play, and master Geoguessr.

Starting a Game of Geoguessr #

You can choose to start a game of Geoguessr with Default Settings or Custom Settings.

Default Settings #

Most players use Default settings. Default settings are a great pick as a beginner.

  1. Open Geoguessr.

  2. Click Classic.

  3. Click on the Map of your choice.

    You could give World a try.

  4. Click Start game! when you are ready.

You are put in a random place in the Map of your choice. Break a leg!

Custom Settings #

Fewer players use Custom settings than Default Settings. Custom settings suit advanced players better.

  1. Open Geoguessr.

  2. Click Classic.

  3. Click on the Map of your choice.

    You could give World a try.

  4. Uncheck Use default settings.

  5. Set a custom Time limit by dragging the handle from left to right.

    By default, the handle is on the far left, which means No time limit. The more you drag it to the right, the more time you add, up to 10 minutes a round.

  6. Set custom Movement settings.

    The default option is Moving, panning and zooming allowed. 4 other options are available:

    • No move
    • No zoom
    • No move, no zoom
    • No move, no pan, no zoom

  7. Click Start game! when you are ready.

You are put in a random place in the Map of your choice. Break a leg!

Using the Compass to pinpoint your location #

You can use the Compass and the Map to identify which street you may be in. American cities, for example, use a grid system, allowing you to identify possible locations. This is less effective in a city constructed in a more organic manner (in Europe for example).

You can see that the Compass points to the Northeast. This means that the Southwest is behind. This rules out about half of the streets of Manhattan Island; the “horizontal” ones, such as 34th Street.

Status #

The Geoguessr Status displays three information:

  • Map displays the current Map.

    For example: “World”.

  • Round displays the current round.

    A round of Geoguessr last 5 rounds.

  • Score displays the current score.

    The maximum score in a game of Geoguessr is 25,000, with 5,000 points per round, depending on the distance to the location.

Panorama #

The Panorama is a feature of Google Maps, called Street View. The Panorama is a feature of Mapillary.

Three actions are available in the Panorama: moving, panning and zooming.

Original location #
Moving #

Moving allows you to go to the next available location.

Panning #

Spanning allows you to rotate on yourself, while staying in the same location.

Zooming #

Zooming allows you to zoom in the Panorama. For example, a billboard here shows the name “Cincinnati”. The text is also in English, indicating you probably are in Cincinnati, or around Cincinnati. Indeed, Cincinnati is in the United States of America.

Movement arrows #

The Movement Arrows are a feature of Google MapsMapillary. An Arrow allows you to navigate through the Panorama in the direction you are facing.

The Panorama changes to the next available image after clicking the Movement Arrows.

North America #

Google Street View covers North America well.

Mexico, as it is closer to the equator, is the most arid. Mexicans speak Spanish.

Canada and the United States of America are harder to distinguish. Both countries speak English, although the province of Québec speaks French.

Climate #

An interesting approach to finding your location is through climate, as it influences flora.

The Southeast of the USA has a hotter climate.

The North, like Canada, has a colder climate.

In the Southwest, the states bordering Mexico are arid. They border the Sonoran and Chihuahuan deserts.

Oceania #

Google Street View does not cover Oceania well.

Google Street View only covers Australia and New Zealand in Oceania.

Both countries speak English and drive on the left-side on the road.

New Zealand is more mountainous than Australia. A large part of Australia, the Outback, is a desert.

Climate #

Regarding climate, New Zealand is colder, and as a consequence “greener”.

Asian Languages #

Recognizing Asian languages is difficult. Let’s explore their writing differences.

Languages of East Asia #

Let’s try distinguishing East Asian countries through their writing:

  1. You can recognize written Korean through two characteristics: it has the most right-angles, and bears a significant amount of ovals and circles.

    Hangul is an alphabet, it has a limited amount of characters.

  2. Written Mandarin, the largest dialect group in China, does not have an alphabet.

    Mandarin has different characters, with unique meanings, which can be assembled to form words. As a consequence, Mandarin characters are dense.

    💡
    Google Street View does not cover mainland China. If you recognize Mandarin, you should be either in Hong Kong or Taiwan. In Hong Kong, as a former British colony, you will spot a lot of English text as well. Taiwan is mountainous in its center, and looks tropical.

  3. You can recognize Japanese because, in comparison to Mandarin, it has simpler characters. They are also more visually open.

Languages of Southeast Asia #

Let’s try distinguishing Southeast Asian countries through their writing:

  1. Thai bears loops or circles in its characters.

  2. If you are familiar with Hindi writing, you will undoubtedly recognize it as similar to Bengali.

    A horizontal bar runs through words in both Bengali and Hindi. However, Google Street View does not cover India.

  3. You can recognize Khmer (the language of Cambodia) through the wave pattern in some of its characters. It also has loops, although some of them are not closed.

  4. Singapore is a linguistically unique place.

    In Singapore, you will find a majority of English, notably in road signs. However, Asian influence shows in the names of streets and districts. This includes names such as “Ang Mo Kio”, “Serangoon Gardens” and “Tiong Bahru”.

    💡
    Singapore appears very frequently in Geoguessr.

  5. The Philippines gained independence from the United States of America in 1946.

    They were previously a colony of Spain. You can recognize the influences of both English and Spanish in the Philippines.

    💡

    A barangay is:

    the smallest administrative division in the Philippines and […] the native Filipino term for a village, district, or ward. Wikipedia

    You can zoom in the Map to find the word abbreviated by “BRGY.” followed by its number.

  1. Lastly, Malay and Indonesian are mutually intelligible. Both languages use the letter a often.

Germanic Languages #

By numbers of native speakers, Germanic languages are German, English, Dutch, Swedish, Danish, Norwegian, Luxembourgish and Icelandic.

Slavic Languages #

There are two groups of Slavic languages: Cyrillic script languages and Latin script languages.

By numbers of native speakers, Cyrillic Slavic languages are Russian, Ukrainian, Belarusian, Bulgarian and Montenegrin.

By numbers of native speakers, Latin Slavic languages are Polish, Czech, Serbian, Slovak, Croatian, Bosnian, Slovene and Macedonian.

Uralic Languages #

By numbers of native speakers, Uralic languages are Hungarian, Finnish and Estonian.

You can recognize Finnish and Estonian through their repeated letters. You can recognize Hungarian through its use of letters “sz” and “ő”.

💡
If you spot a lot of double letters and the letters “õ” and “ü”, you should be looking at Estonian.
Baltic Languages #

By numbers of native speakers, Baltic languages are Lithuanian and Latvian.

💡
In European languages, Latvian is the only language to have the letter “ī” (an “i” with a horizontal bar).

Spanish vs. Portuguese #

If, at first glance, Spanish and Portuguese seem similar, they do have differences.

Portuguese has distinctive diacritics: for example, the cedilla ç, as well as circumflex accents on â, ê and ô. The tilde is only found in Spanish in ñ while Portuguese has it on ã and õ. It is present in the name of the most populated city in Brazil: São Paulo.

Meta Clues #

The nature of imagery makes it that by country, or region, you can spot meta clues.

Meta clues are any recurring sign originating from the creation of the Panorama. For example, when you can see the car that captured the image.

Roof Racks #

In Geoguessr, when you rotate the camera downwards, you can sometimes see a roof rack in the Panorama.

Roof racks are a recurring motif in African countries. Roof racks hold the 360° degree camera that actually captures the Panorama.

⚠️
Not all images from a given country are taken from the same car. Try to find other hints.
Ghana #

In Ghana, for example, the roof rack has black tape on one of its sides.

Nigeria #

In Nigeria, the roof rack has black-and-yellow tape.

Kenya #

In Kenya, the roof rack bears no significant hint. However, on the left front of the car you can spot a protruding arm.

Two non-African countries use a roof rack: Mongolia and Kyrgyzstan.

Mongolia #

In Mongolia, the roof rack has two beams crossing the racks.

Kyrgyzstan #

In Kyrgyzstan, the roof rack has no distinguishing feature. Try to find other hints.

In the following case, moving the camera around reveals Arabic script. Arabic is the traditional way of writing in Kyrgyz, which belongs to the Turkic languages.

I Can’t Play Geoguessr! #

Here are a few common issues while playing Geoguessr, and our solutions.

The Geoguessr Panorama Displays a Black Screen #

geoguessr only shows me a black screen, aaaaaaaggghhhh!!!!!!!!

Everything is going to be okay, you only have insufficient connection speed. Try the following course of action:

  1. Begin by refreshing your tab.

    • On Windows, this feature is next to the Back and Forward buttons. This applies to most web browsers, including Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox and Microsoft Edge.
    💡
    You can also press F5 or CTRL+R.
    • On Mac OS, if you are using Safari, this feature is next to the Search bar.
    💡
    You can also press Command+R.
  1. If the previous step did not solve your issue, consider reading Geoguessr is inaccessible.

Geoguessr Is Inaccessible #

i can’t reach geoguessr.com anymore! pls halp!

It seems you are not connected to the Internet anymore. Try the following course of action:

  1. Begin by checking your Network settings.

    • On Windows, go to Start Menu > Settings > Network & internet.
    • On Mac OS, go to Apple menu > System Preferences > Network.

  2. If you’re indeed disconnected, try the following course of action:

    • If you are connected through wifi, try turning off then on the wifi option.
    • If you’re connected through Ethernet, remove the cable from your computer then plug the cable again.

  3. If the previous step did not solve your issue, turn your computer off then on.

  4. If the previous step did not solve your issue, check your router.

    A red blinking red light signals a problem with the connection of your router.

  5. If your router does seem to be malfunctioning, turn it off then on.

  6. If after turning your router off then on the issue persists, call your Internet service provider for assistance.

You can choose to limit yourself in order to make the game harder, with one of the following restrictions:

NM: No moving #

In a NM (No Moving) game, you cannot move. However, you can pan or zoom. NM makes use of Custom Settings.

NMPZ: No moving, panning or zooming #

In a NMPZ (No Moving Panning or Zooming) game, you cannot move, pan or zoom. NMPZ makes use of Custom Settings.

This is the hardest way of playing Geoguessr, and the most prestigious.

No player has ever had a perfect game (25,000 points) playing this way.

NCNC: No Car No Compass #

You can choose to apply scripts to Geoguessr to make the game harder. For example, in “NCNC” or “No Car No Compass” you choose to gray out the car. You cannot get a clue from it (see Meta Clues).

👉
You can combine NCNC with NM and NMPZ.

Conclusion #

What makes Geoguessr interesting is that there is no easy way to become good at it.

Contrary to many games, Geoguessr does not require dexterity, or an advanced knowledge of its mechanics to be a good player. Geoguessr also does not require you to play for hours to have some better weapons, or in-game skills.

You can of course learn patterns and clues inherent to Geoguessr. However, what makes Geoguessr so good is that it is all about curiosity; and our innate desire to know the world, and others.

Thank you for your time!

Grow into an Internet country domains expert #

You may be saying: what is that again?

Web addresses often appear on billboards and store fronts, particularly in urban environments. They all end with a top-level domain, such as .com.

Each country receives its own top-level domain, which often helps to determine what country you are in. They are often quite obvious; .fr is for France and .ru is for Russia. However, some top-level domains can require you to learn them.

For example, .za stands for Zuid-Afrika, meaning South-Africa. .dz stands for Al Dzayer, meaning Algeria.

💡
A good resource is the website WorldStandards.
